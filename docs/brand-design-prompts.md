# Subtext Brand Design Prompts

A collection of brand direction and lifestyle/moodboard prompts for graphic design exploration.

---

## Product Overview (For Designer Context)

**Subtext** is a privacy-first, on-device dating conversation coach for Gen Z (18-27). The app helps users communicate more authentically in dating/relationship conversations—all while keeping their conversations completely private on their iPhone.

**Core Values:**
- Privacy (conversations never leave the device)
- Authenticity (coaching, not scripting)
- Safety (red flag detection)
- Empowerment (become better communicators)
- Confidence without arrogance

**Target Audience:** Gen Z daters who are mobile-first, value authenticity, concerned about privacy, and want meaningful connections.

## Brand Direction 1: "Calm Confidence"

### Concept
A serene, sophisticated aesthetic that evokes quiet confidence. Think of the feeling of being prepared—knowing exactly what to say without stress. Minimal, breathable, and mature without being boring.

### Moodboard Prompt
```
Create a moodboard for a dating conversation coach app targeting Gen Z.
Style: Calm confidence, sophisticated minimalism.
Color palette: Soft neutrals (warm whites, soft grays), paired with a single
accent color—muted sage green or dusty lavender.
Typography: Clean sans-serif with generous spacing, perhaps with one elegant
serif for headlines.
Imagery: Soft natural light, quiet morning moments, a person thoughtfully
looking at their phone with a slight smile, coffee shop corners, soft-focus
urban environments at golden hour.
Mood: Serene, prepared, quietly confident, unhurried.
Visual elements: Flowing organic shapes, subtle gradients, soft shadows,
plenty of negative space.
Avoid: Loud colors, aggressive gradients, tech/corporate feel, cluttered
compositions.
```

### Logo Direction Prompt
```
Design a wordmark logo for "Subtext" - a privacy-first dating conversation
coach app.
Style: Refined minimalism with a subtle clever twist.
Concept directions:
1. Emphasize the "sub" in subtext with a subtle underline or baseline element
2. Incorporate negative space to suggest hidden meaning or layers
3. Use letterform that suggests conversation (speech bubbles abstracted)
Color: Works in single color (sage green or deep gray), can be inverted
Feel: Premium, trustworthy, approachable, clever but not gimmicky
Reference aesthetic: Headspace, Notion, Calm app
```

## Lifestyle Photography Direction

### General Lifestyle Prompt
```
Create a lifestyle photography moodboard for Subtext's marketing.
Subject: Gen Z (18-27), diverse in ethnicity, gender expression, body types.
Settings: Urban coffee shops, apartment living rooms, transit moments,
evening rooftops, casual hangouts, solo moments with phones.
Moments to capture:
- Thoughtfully composing a message with a slight smile
- The relief of sending a message you feel good about
- Confident glances at phone screens
- Comfortable solitude that feels chosen, not lonely
- Friends together but comfortable in their own spaces
Style: Editorial quality but authentic—not stock, not overly posed.
Lighting: Natural, golden hour preferred, some intentional night/screen light.
Avoid: Stereotypical dating imagery (red roses, hearts), couple shots (focus
on individual empowerment), anxious/stressed expressions, over-filtered.
```

### Diverse Representation Guidelines
```
Photography casting and representation guidelines for Subtext brand:
- Gender: All gender expressions, not predominantly cis-het presenting
- Ethnicity: Truly diverse—not tokenized, reflects actual Gen Z demographics
- Body types: Range of body types presented equally
- Style: Various fashion expressions from minimalist to expressive
- Settings: Mix of urban, suburban, various economic contexts
- Ability: Include visible and non-visible disabilities naturally
- Relationships: Include imagery relevant to LGBTQ+ dating
Priority: Authentic representation over manufactured diversity
```
